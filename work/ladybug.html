Folding for Outer Space



A mock cover for Nature magazine, based on a 2017 research study conducted at the University of Tokyo that determined the folding mechanism of ladybug wings for the first time. The study's findings explain how the wings can maintain great strength and rigidity during flight, while also having the impressive ability to elastically fold for compact storage.

A visual metaphor of an origami ladybug was used to emphasize the unique folding qualities of their wings, and to convey the scientists' hopes to apply these lessons to designing space-deployable structures such as satellite antennas.
