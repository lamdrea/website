Kidney-on-a-Chip

Master's Research Project


Kidneys are an important organ in our body, but they are particularly difficult to study in developing treatments for kidney-related illnesses. As a result, researchers are working towards developing a functional 'in vitro' model of the kidneyâ€”the term 'in vitro' refers to cells/organisms/molecules that are studied on outside of its natural environment. A successful, functioning in vitro kidney model would have huge implications for the public, including faster drug development, a diminished need for animal models, and personalized medicine.

Biomedical engineers at the University of Toronto achieved a big milestone in engineering specialized kidney cells called podocytes. This animation was created so that the clients could communicate and promote their research to both a lay and research audience.

Storyboards

Goals: My clients wanted an animation that they could flexibly use depending who their audience was. Their first goal was to have visuals to accompany their research at conferences with peers and stakeholders, while the second goal was to promote their field to the general public.

Solution: Depending on who the audience is, my clients could clip this animation to suit their needs, while also being able to play it as a whole piece. The animation is sectioned into 4 chapters, where chapters 1 & 4 introduces the problem and dives into the long-term implications of their field, while chapters 2 & 3 contains the science and research findings. This is also why there are stylistic differences, where 1 & 4 are more simple and playful for a general audience, whereas 2 & 3 are more photorealistic to appeal to peers, investors and stakeholders.
